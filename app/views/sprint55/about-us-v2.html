Who is the National Careers Service for?

Citizens

We provide citizens aged 13+ with careers information, advice and guidance to help you make good career choices.

Intermediaries

We provide information for teachers, parents and advisers to support others in their career journey.

Stakeholders

We work with employers across all sectors to make sure that the information we provide is representative of the labour market.

Digital careers information and advice

The National Careers Website is here to give you the tools to make decisions about your career at your own pace and in your own time. You can:

  • browse our job profiles for inspiration
  • read careers advice articles
  • take a skills assessment
  • find a course
  • update your skills

Telephone and web chat support

If you want some extra support on your career journey, you can contact us to speak to an adviser or start a web chat. Our careers advisers can help you work out where you want to go in your career and give you the techniques to get you there.

Face to face careers guidance

You can also access 1-1 careers guidance through the National Careers Service. Trained careers advisers can help you to build your confidence to take your next steps on your career journey.

If you contact an adviser for support, our customer charter will help you understand what to expect from your meeting.

U.K. careers services

This service is available to people who live in England. Careers services are also available for:

  • Scotland
  • Northern Ireland
  • Wales
